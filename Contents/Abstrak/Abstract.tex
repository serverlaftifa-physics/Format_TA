\textbf{Abstract}
\par Modern financial market stability is frequently distorted by extreme correlation phenomena and non-linear dynamics that cannot be accurately accommodated by conventional \textit{Mean-Variance} models. This proposal suggests a new investment portfolio optimization framework through the integration of econophysics principles, game theory, and variational quantum computing. The asset allocation problem is transformed into an \textit{Ising Hamiltonian} structure using the \textit{Quadratic Unconstrained Binary Optimization} (QUBO) formulation to map the collective behavior of economic agents represented as a system of \textit{spins}. \textit{Marginal payoff} from strategic utility is used to determine local field parameters ($h_i$) and the implementation of \textit{Quantum Mutual Information} (QMI) in constructing coupling coefficients ($J_{ij}$) to capture non-linear information dependencies between assets. The search for the optimal portfolio configuration or \textit{ground state} is performed using the \textit{Variational Quantum Eigensolver} (VQE) algorithm with the \textit{EfficientSU(2)} ansatz and \textit{Simultaneous Perturbation Stochastic Approximation} (SPSA) optimization technique. Evaluation is conducted on multi-sector historical data over five years to test the model's resilience against market volatility.
